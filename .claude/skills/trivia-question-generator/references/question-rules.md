# Question Writing Rules

## Difficulty Guidelines

### Easy
- Common knowledge most adults would know
- Famous people, capitals, well-known events, basic science
- Single clear fact being tested

### Medium
- Requires some study or general interest in the topic
- Less famous but notable facts, second-tier knowledge
- May require combining two facts

### Hard
- Specialist or enthusiast-level knowledge
- Obscure facts, precise dates, technical details, lesser-known figures

## Question Rules

DO:
- Use clear direct language
- Test one specific fact per question
- Make the question self-contained

DO NOT:
- Use double negatives
- Write trick questions relying on ambiguous wording
- Use questions whose answer depends on when asked (current president, etc.)
- Make the correct answer obviously longer than distractors

## Distractor Rules

Good distractors are:
- Plausible — fit the type of answer expected
- Wrong — unambiguously incorrect
- Non-obvious — at least one close-but-no answer
- Similar format — same type as correct answer

## Explanation Rules

Each explanation should:
1. Confirm why the correct answer is correct
2. Add 1-2 sentences of interesting context
3. Optionally note why the most tempting wrong answer is wrong
4. Be 2-4 sentences maximum

## Default Platform Categories
- General Knowledge
- Film & TV
- Music
- Geography
- History
- Sports
- Science & Nature
- Arts & Literature

## Difficulty Distribution
For N questions (always multiple of 3):
- Easy: N divided by 3
- Medium: N divided by 3
- Hard: N divided by 3
